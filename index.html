About

I am a Ph.D. student in the Computer Science Department at the University of California, Irvine. Padhraic Smyth is my advisor. I am broadly intereted in developpig new methods for quantifiying and reducing uncertainty of models with techniques from probabilistic learning and statistics.

In particular, I am passionate about reliability and fairness of machine learning systems, and applications to healthcare. Recent projects I worked on:

  • reliable and label-efficient assessment of fairness with Bayesian methods [to appear]
  • assessment of deep learning models with Bayesian active learning[3]
  • automated diagnosis of Leukemia (accuracy and interpretability on par with human expertise!) [2]
  • automated cytometry data analysis with Bayesian trees [1]

I've interned at Google Cambridge and Facebook New York. I obtained my undergrad degree in mathematics from Fudan University (Shanghai, China) in 2015.
