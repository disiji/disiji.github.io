About

I am a Ph.D. student in computer science at the University of California, Irvine. Padhraic Smyth is my advisor. I am broadly intereted in developing new methods for quantifying and reducing uncertainty of models with techniques from probabilistic learning and statistics.

In particular, I am passionate about reliability and fairness of machine learning systems, and their applications to healthcare. Recent projects I worked on:

  • human-machine collaboration for image annotation [coming soon]
  • reliable and label-efficient assessment of fairness with Bayesian methods [3]
  • assessment of deep learning models with Bayesian active learning[4]
  • automated diagnosis of Leukemia (accuracy and interpretability on par with human expertise!) [2]
  • cell-level cytometry data analysis with Bayesian trees [1]

I interned at Google Cambridge and Facebook New York. I obtained my undergrad degree in mathematics and applied mathematics from Fudan University (Shanghai, China) in 2015.
